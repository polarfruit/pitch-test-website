Why Pitch.

Every name decision has a reason. This one has five.

Pitch.

The Vendor's Pitch
You're pitching yourself to events. The name mirrors the core action on the platform — putting yourself forward, making your case.

✓ Do
  • Always include the trailing period — it is part of the name
  • Use Fraunces 900 weight exclusively for the wordmark
  • Use on coal, parchment, or Brand Ember backgrounds only
  • Maintain minimum clearspace of 1× cap-height on all sides
  • Use the wordmark-only version (no icon needed)
✗ Don't
  • Don't remove the period — "Pitch" without it is not the logo
  • Don't use a different typeface, weight, or letter-spacing
  • Don't place on complex photographic backgrounds
  • Don't add gradients, shadows, or outlines to the wordmark
  • Don't stretch, rotate, or distort the mark in any direction

Why These Colours — The Psychology
Warm Darks, Not Cold Black
Pure black (#000) reads as corporate, harsh, and digital. Our Coal (#1A1612) has a 30° warm brown hue — the colour of chalkboard menus, wooden market stalls, and fire pits. It makes food feel at home on the screen.
Parchment Over White
White backgrounds trigger "clinical" and "sterile" associations — great for hospitals, terrible for food. Parchment (#FDF4E7) evokes handwritten menus, craft food packaging, and artisan markets. It makes vendors feel seen.
Ember Orange vs. Bright Orange
We pulled the primary slightly deeper and warmer than typical app orange. Bright orange can read as cheap. Ember orange reads as fire, heat, and quality — like a wood-fired oven, not a fast food sign.
Herb Green, Not Spotify Green
#1DB954 reads as "tech platform". Our Herb Green (#2D8B55) is slightly cooler and more muted — it reads as fresh produce, basil, and approval. It triggers a food association, not a streaming app.
Gold Over Yellow Amber
Bright amber yellows trigger anxiety and cheapness. Our Gold (#C9840A) is deep and saturated — it reads as a golden hour warning, not a system alarm. It keeps the platform feeling premium even in cautionary states.
Terracotta, Not Alarm Red
Digital red (#FF0000) is jarring and aggressive. Terracotta (#C0392B) is earth-toned — it communicates "stop" or "error" without triggering panic. It keeps the platform warm even when delivering bad news to a vendor.
03 · Typography

The Type System

One serif for character. One sans for clarity. Both chosen for how they make people feel, not just how they look.

Fraunces · Display & Logo
An "optical size" serif — it was designed to feel completely different at large and small sizes. At display sizes it's expressive, almost poetic. It conveys craft, warmth, and confidence. Used for the logo, all headings, and the hero text. Chosen because it feels like a handpainted market sign scaled up.

Instrument Sans · Body & UI
A contemporary geometric sans that's warm rather than cold. Unlike Inter or Roboto which read as "software", Instrument Sans has humanist details that keep it approachable. Used for all body copy, buttons, labels, navigation, and form fields. Legible at 11px, elegant at 18px.

07 · Motion & Spacing

Motion Principles

🎪
Entrance
ease-out · 280ms
Cards and panels entering the screen. Subtle Y-axis rise + opacity fade from 0 → 1. Never bouncy — confident.
✨
Transition
ease-in-out · 200ms
Page changes, tab switches, overlay fades. Smooth and unhurried — the platform knows where it's going.
→
Micro
ease · 140ms
Button hovers, badge updates, fill bar fills on load. Fast, responsive, invisible — just feels right.

08 · Voice & Tone

How Pitch Speaks

The visual brand is only half of it. How Pitch speaks to vendors and organizers must match the warmth, confidence, and directness of the visual identity.

Direct
"Apply now. 6 spots left."
Never bury the action. Tell people what to do and why now. Vendors are busy — respect their time. No corporate waffle.
Confident
"Your pitch. Owned."
Pitch makes vendors feel capable. The copy lifts them, not the platform. We don't say "we help you find" — we say "find your pitch."
Warm
"Welcome back, Joe."
Every vendor is a person with a craft they're proud of. Pitch recognises that. Never clinical, never robotic — always human.
